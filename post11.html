Disabled-friendly Apple Cutter

This project was started in my freshman year in college in which I helped invent, innovate, and design a special apple cutter device for a disabled-client.

The client was Sheila, a long-term care resident at St. Peter's Residence at Chedoke who had suffered a stroke resulting in limited mobility and use of her left arm and leg.

She required a device that would allow her to easily cut apples while using one hand that is also safe, easily maintained and cleanred, and accessible for her and the staff of the long-term care home.

Following a complicated engineering design process involving many milestone presentations, reports and metric evaluation timelines I successfully placed top 3 class-wide in the freshman year engineering competition alongside 3 other colleagues.
