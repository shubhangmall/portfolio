The Re-Design of CD-ROM Drive

Cornerstone Design Project

This project was started in my freshman year at college where I was tasked to design a gear-train that will use a new motor to drive the read-head while producing the same industry-standard read-head movement and speed.

The original mechanism is assumed to be unavailable; the new motor will be in a completely different location with limited design space.

The design must include a compound gear train occupying area of stage A, a worm-rack drive occuping stage B, and a custom-designed mounting bracket to hold all the components together.

All the gears and custom components were modelled in Autodesk Inventor. The mechanism must be constrained to the model of the CD-ROM drive and scaled at 1:1 ratio.

The sytem was modelled using MapleSim to demonstrate that the input and output speeds match the requirements. A test block that simulates the oscillating operation of the read head must be able to be properly connected to the simulated model.

Finally, the customized components were 3D printed and then assembled to demonstrate their functionality.
